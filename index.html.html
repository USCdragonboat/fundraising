Core philosophy
  • Keep costs low — the team holds many fundraisers and goodwill with the dragon boat community is essential. Don't alienate people with high prices or poor quality.
  • Combo deals (e.g. 2 for $5) encourage more sales, bring friends, and make customers happier.
  • Ensure quality control by being physically present at every event. Lead and direct volunteers — food/drinks without oversight will likely turn out poorly.
  • Learn from every event — write down what went well, what went wrong, and how to fix it.
  • Release periodic fundraising updates: "We've raised $X — X% of our goal. These funds will go toward Tempe race dues." Thank people and show the money goes back to them.
Before the semester starts
  • Coordinate with PR Chair for flyer designs — do this well in advance.
  • Align with eboard so fundraisers don't clash with GMs or practices.
  • Ask Keith if adult teams will be at practice (affects attendance and what days work).
  • Get UCLA Dragon Boat fundraising chair's phone number — Instagram DMs are too slow. Avoid scheduling on the same day as their fundraisers.
  • Avoid major holidays and big events (Super Bowl, Easter, etc.).
  • Check the fundraising folder in the DB email for past templates and references.

Table fundraisers

Mother's Beach ✓ Preferred
  • Monopoly on food/drinks — often the only seller.
  • Paddlers hungry/thirsty after practice — captive audience.
  • Older, more generous crowd — tips are common.
  • Much higher sales overall.
Trousdale — avoid
  • Mostly dragon boat members and their friends buying.
  • Much lower revenue.
  • Poor organic foot traffic.
General tips
  • Find cheap ingredients — Asian grocery stores are often cheaper for relevant items.
  • Be selective about helpers — some people can't cook, are clumsy, or aren't reliable with cash.
  • Bring lots of cash change: $5s and $1s. People pay with $20s. Have a clear cash tracking system.
  • Bring stickers to sell at the table — easy upsell that boosts revenue.
  • Print a color sticker menu at Leavey (~50 cents). Avoid prices with cents unless you have coin change.
Menu items
Portos Easiest
  • Buy and pick up from Downey Portos — not far from Mother's Beach.
  • Order the night before, after 10pm only. Separate pre-orders from walk-in inventory clearly.
  • Has sold out in the past (except at Trousdale) — order generously.
Thai Tea Hotter days
  • Needs: big jug, multiple large pots, tupperware for boba and simple syrup. Do NOT pour very hot tea into the plastic jug — it melts.
  • Takes a long time — mostly boiling and steeping for hours. Plan well ahead.
  • Need helpers at the table for scooping, pouring, and taking orders. Recipe in Thai Tea folder in DB email.
Spam Musubi Group activity
  • Use an assembly line. Make the night before, refrigerate, then microwave briefly in the morning (2 at a time, wet paper towel on top).
  • Keep in a cooler bag on the day — they stay warm. Easy to sell: customers just grab them.
Lemonade Hotter days
  • Similar setup to Thai Tea but much less prep — no long steeping or boiling required.
  • Ingredients: concentrated lemon juice, simple syrup, water, ice. Add lemon slices as decor for visual appeal.
  • Easy to make flavored variations on the fly — add mango, strawberry, or other juice/syrup to create a menu of options. Great upsell opportunity.
  • Needs the same big jug and cooler setup as Thai Tea. Great sales on hot days.
Banh Mi Lunch option
  • Order the day before or early morning — shop opens at 5am, so you can pick up on the way to practice.
  • Location: 8940 Westminster Blvd., Westminster, CA 92683 (Albert's favorite spot).
  • Always offer a combo deal (2 for $x) — portions are large so you can price more aggressively.
  • Massive profit margin — one of the best revenue-per-dollar options on the menu.
Thai Curry Pizza Lunch option
  • Located close to Mother's Beach — convenient pickup.
  • Easy option: call during or after practice to place the order. Minimal prep or logistics required.
Always include at least one vegetarian option — but don't over-order vegetarian items since they're harder to sell. A small portion of the menu goes a long way.
Stickers
  • Order through Sticker App — vinyl/matte. Bring to every table fundraiser and race.
  • At races: gather extroverts and sell to USC and other teams. Adult teams are more generous.
  • Avoid prices with cents unless you have coin change.

Outreach & sponsorships

Donations — race support
  • Starbucks is usually willing to donate coffee for races — may need to call multiple nearby locations.
  • Must pick up the morning of the race. Coffee tastes bad if picked up the day before.
  • Other potential donors: bakeries, restaurants, grocery stores.
  • May need 501(c) or W9 documentation to prove non-profit status.
  • Always send a thank-you email after receiving any donation.
Cold call script
Ask to speak to a manager before using this script.
"Hello, I'm calling on behalf of the USC Dragon Boat team — we are a nonprofit university sports club. We have a race in [location] on [day and date] and we were wondering if [business] was interested in donating [item] to help support us."
Sponsorship tips
  • Target smaller/emerging brands trying to grow their marketing reach. Big brands are unlikely to prioritize small student clubs.
  • Past targets: Celsius (successful — 120 cans), PROBAR, Red Bull, Gatorade (limited success with large brands so far).

Crowdfunding & institutional funding

Ignite crowdfunding
  • Point of contact must be a club officer. Coordinate directly with RCC for setup.
  • RCC has historically been slow — confirm timelines and hold them accountable. Supposed to do one campaign per semester.
  • Do not use other crowdfunding platforms — RCC advises against it.
  • Do NOT use "USC Dragonboat" in campaign text — use "Dragon Boat @SC" to avoid copyright/licensing issues.
Campaign script
Dragon Boat @SC is dedicated to fostering an inclusive community, but rising costs are making it challenging for many of our members to participate. With travel expenses to races in San Francisco and Arizona (~$3,000–$5,000 each), boat rentals for practice (over $5,000), and membership dues, financial barriers are becoming a reality. To avoid increasing membership dues, we're seeking fundraising support. Contributions will help lower race costs and general membership costs to ensure every member can train and compete without financial strain.

Creative fundraisers

These are high-energy, community-driven fundraisers that generate buzz beyond just revenue — great for team culture and visibility.
🥧
Pie-a-Bruin
Conquest Week
  • Run during Conquest Week for maximum energy and foot traffic — the rivalry context makes it especially popular.
  • Bought a bear costume and a pie maker. The setup is reusable season to season.
  • Was extremely well-received — even got covered by the news.
🎯
Bingo
Social media-driven
  • Share the bingo card on social media — reach extends well beyond the team.
  • Feature someone popular or well-known in the DB community (e.g. Joshua Evan Fendi) as the subject. The more recognizable, the more engagement.
  • Set creative, funny bingo objectives with tiered prices (see reference card).
  • Fulfill requests on race days or major events when the whole community is gathered.
Tips from the reference card
  • Include a mix of price points — from $2 (easy dares) up to $50 (wild ones).
  • Add a free center square to lower the barrier to engagement.
  • Collect via Venmo (@USCDB) — include paddler name and the specific dare in the note.
  • Mix physical dares, social media dares, and food challenges for variety.
